Dear Data Access Team,

I am requesting access to restricted-use **National Crime Victimization Survey
(NCVS)** files containing **sub-national geographic identifiers** (metropolitan
statistical area and/or county where available) for survey years **[2018–2023]**.

**Purpose.** I am conducting methodological research on estimating latent
victimization rates from multiple *mechanism-independent* measurement channels
(household victimization survey, police-recorded crime, and 911 calls-for-
service) under a repeated-measurement deconvolution and conformal-prediction
framework. NCVS serves as the near-unbiased, non-police measurement channel that
captures victimization *not reported to police*, and is essential for validating
a latent-rate estimator against an independent source. Sub-national geography is
required to align NCVS with metro-level police records and calls-for-service.

**Outputs and disclosure.** All reported quantities are **area-level aggregates**
at the survey's native geographic resolution. **No individual-level
re-identification is attempted or possible in any output**; I will comply with
all cell-suppression and minimum-count disclosure rules.

**Compliance materials I can provide upon request:**
- an **IRB determination** from [your institution's IRB / an independent IRB]
  (secondary analysis of de-identified survey data; expected "exempt" or
  "not human-subjects research" determination);
- a **data-security plan** (encrypted storage, access controls, no cloud copies
  of restricted files, deletion schedule);
- a signed **Restricted Data Use Agreement** and any required researcher
  affidavits.

Please advise on the applicable process (NACJD Restricted Data Use Agreement vs.
a Federal Statistical Research Data Center proposal), required documentation,
fees if any, and expected timeline. I am happy to complete any additional forms.

Thank you for your time and for maintaining this vital public resource.
